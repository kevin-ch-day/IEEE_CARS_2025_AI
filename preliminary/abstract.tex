\begin{abstract}
This paper presents a defensive, controlled-study framework for comparing baseline and
AI-assisted malware-analysis workflows. We do not attempt to infer real-world AI authorship
from compiled artifacts; instead, we evaluate whether explicitly defined workflow conditions
produce measurable differences in static indicators, behavioral traces, and detection outcomes.
The study design uses matched tasks, explicit provenance labeling, and consistent analysis
practices to produce defensible evidence under controlled conditions. The results section is
structured to separate observable findings from interpretation, with emphasis on which metrics
support practical defensive decisions. By focusing on reproducibility, safety boundaries, and
limitations, this work is intended as a foundation for responsible cybersecurity research that
supports improved analysis and detection readiness without enabling misuse.
\end{abstract}
