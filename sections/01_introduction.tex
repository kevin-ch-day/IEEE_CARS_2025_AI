\section{Introduction}

Artificial intelligence tools are increasingly used in software development, cybersecurity education, and defensive research workflows. These tools can assist with code explanation, documentation, debugging, test generation, and security analysis. At the same time, their availability raises questions about how AI-assisted workflows may affect malware development, malware modification, and defensive detection.

This paper studies AI assistance from a defensive research perspective. Rather than making direct claims about whether real-world malware was authored by an AI system, the study focuses on observable artifacts, controlled workflow differences, and measurable defensive outcomes.

The contributions of this paper are as follows:
\begin{itemize}
    \item We define a controlled study design for evaluating AI-assisted malware-research workflows.
    \item We identify measurable artifacts that can be compared across baseline and AI-assisted conditions.
    \item We discuss defensive implications for malware analysis, detection engineering, and cybersecurity education.
\end{itemize}
