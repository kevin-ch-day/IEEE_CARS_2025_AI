\section{Literature Review}

\subsection{Static Android Exposure and Analysis Limitations}
Static Android research has measured manifest errors, permission misuse, component exposure, and tool effectiveness at scale. ManiScope demonstrated that manifest misconfiguration remains common across Google Play and pre-installed apps \cite{yang2022maniscope}. Wang et al.\ showed that runtime permission handling is still error-prone across Android versions and execution states \cite{wang2023runtimepermissions}. Zhu et al.\ provide a recent systematic evaluation of Android SAST tools and show that vulnerability-type coverage and reporting consistency vary substantially \cite{zhu2024sastandroid}. These results motivate static measurement while reinforcing that static indicators require careful interpretation.

\subsection{Runtime and Network Observation}
Dynamic Android analysis can reveal behavior that is absent or ambiguous in packaged artifacts. A systematic review by Sutter et al.\ identifies coverage, stimulation strategy, nondeterminism, and environment dependence as recurring limits \cite{sutter2024dynamic}. Network-observation studies similarly show that execution-time evidence is needed to characterize service activation and third-party tracking behavior \cite{paci2023tracking}. This paper follows that boundary: runtime captures provide scenario-bounded metadata, not exhaustive behavior or decrypted content.

\subsection{Privacy Disclosures and Third-Party SDK Behavior}
Recent work on Google Play Data Safety labels shows that disclosure systems and actual app behavior may diverge. Arkalakis et al.\ dynamically analyzed app behavior against Data Safety disclosures and found widespread incomplete disclosure \cite{arkalakis2024datasafety}. Khandelwal et al.\ studied Data Safety labels from both measurement and developer perspectives, showing over-reporting, under-reporting, and developer friction \cite{khandelwal2024privacylabels}. Zhang et al.\ examined privacy-configurable mobile SDKs and found systematic risks in how SDK privacy APIs are implemented and used \cite{zhang2024privacysdks}. These studies motivate treating disclosures and SDK indicators as context rather than substitutes for technical evidence.

\subsection{Combined Static-Runtime Studies}
Combined studies are valuable because static and runtime evidence answer different questions. Karyotakis et al.\ recently compared Android messaging apps with static and dynamic scenarios \cite{karyotakis2026messaging}. The present work differs by expanding to a heterogeneous 15-app dataset, preserving selected-build provenance across static and runtime evidence, and explicitly separating strict idle, QFG, and interactive captures.

Prior work commonly provides strong static, runtime, disclosure, or SDK-specific analysis, but less often combines exact-build-aligned static and runtime evidence with explicit app-version provenance, QFG separation, and a fixed recent-evidence window across a heterogeneous consumer-app dataset.
