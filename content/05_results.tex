Static exposure varied substantially across apps. \subsection{App-Level Divergence and Representative Cases}
Snapchat is the clearest high-static case: the selected build has high static finding volume, but runtime interpretation depends on limited strict idle coverage and QFG/interactive separation. Facebook combines high static finding volume with broad observed runtime domain activity. CNN has lower static exposure than the large social apps but strong runtime evidence depth and interactive traffic broadening. Signal and Telegram show lower observed service diversity in the selected captures, consistent with narrower encrypted messaging behavior under the exercised scenarios.

The prior runtime bridge had an apparent discrepancy: aggregate metric-shift text referred to nine compared apps, while one detailed table listed eight app rows. The current generator resolves this by exporting a baseline-relative eligibility CSV for every app. Baseline-relative comparisons are now reported only with explicit eligibility/exclusion state and should not be read as covering every app unless the eligibility CSV says so.
